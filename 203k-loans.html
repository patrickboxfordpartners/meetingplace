Common Questions

203k Loan FAQ

How much can I borrow with a 203k loan?

The maximum loan amount is based on FHA lending limits for your county, which typically range from $498,257 to $1,149,825 for a single-family home. The loan must include both the purchase price and renovation costs.

Can I live in the home during renovations?

For Limited 203k loans with minor cosmetic work, you can typically live in the home. For Standard 203k loans with major structural work, you may need to find temporary housing during construction, depending on the scope of work and local safety requirements.

Do I choose the contractor or does the lender?

You choose your own contractor! However, they must be licensed, insured, and willing to work within the 203k program guidelines. For Standard 203k, you'll also need an FHA-approved consultant to oversee the project.

What if the renovation costs more than estimated?

It's important to have accurate estimates upfront. If costs exceed the loan amount, you'll need to cover the difference out of pocket. A 10-20% contingency reserve is typically built into the loan for unexpected issues.

How long does it take to close on a 203k loan?

203k loans typically take 45-60 days to close due to the additional documentation, appraisals, and work specifications required. Limited 203k loans are usually faster than Standard 203k loans.

Can I do the work myself to save money?

DIY work is limited. You can do some non-structural work like painting or landscaping, but you cannot pay yourself. All major work must be done by licensed contractors. This protects both you and the lender by ensuring quality work.

Understanding 203k

What Is a 203k Loan?

The FHA 203k loan is a government-backed mortgage that allows you to finance both the purchase (or refinance) of a house and the cost of its rehabilitation through a single mortgage. Instead of taking out two separate loans—one for the home purchase and another for renovations—everything is bundled into one convenient package.

This program is perfect for buyers who want to:

Buy a fixer-upper below market value
Customize and renovate before moving in
Build equity immediately through improvements
Loan Types

Two 203k Options to Choose From

Standard 203k

MAJOR PROJECTS

For extensive renovations requiring structural repairs or improvements over $35,000. Best for major remodels and homes needing significant work.

Renovation Range:

$35,000+

Ideal For:

  • Structural repairs (foundation, roof)
  • Room additions or major remodels
  • Complete kitchen/bathroom renovations
  • HVAC, plumbing, electrical upgrades

Note: Requires FHA-approved consultant to oversee work and draw inspections.

Limited 203k

STREAMLINED

A simpler, faster process for cosmetic updates and non-structural improvements under $35,000. Perfect for move-in ready updates.

Renovation Range:

Up to $35,000

Ideal For:

  • Painting, flooring, new appliances
  • New cabinets, countertops, fixtures
  • Minor repairs and updates
  • Landscaping and energy efficiency

Note: No consultant required. Faster approval and simpler process.
