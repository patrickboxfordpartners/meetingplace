Program Overview

What Is a USDA Loan?

USDA loans are mortgage products backed by the U.S. Department of Agriculture designed to promote homeownership in rural and suburban communities. The program's most attractive feature is 100% financing—no down payment required—making homeownership accessible to buyers who might not have significant savings.

Despite the name "rural," USDA loans are available in more areas than you might think. Many suburban communities, small towns, and areas on the outskirts of major cities qualify. The property must be in a USDA-eligible area, and buyers must meet income requirements.

Credit & DTI

  • 640+ credit score recommended
  • Manual underwriting for 620-639
  • 41% maximum DTI ratio
  • U.S. citizenship or permanent residency

Additional Qualifications

The property must be your primary residence (no investment properties or second homes). The home must meet USDA property standards and pass a home inspection. Both new construction and existing homes qualify, provided they meet safety and livability requirements.

Common Questions

USDA Loan FAQ

Can I really buy a house with no money down?

Yes! USDA loans offer 100% financing, meaning no down payment is required. You'll still need funds for closing costs, though sellers can contribute up to 6% of the purchase price toward these costs, significantly reducing your out-of-pocket expenses.

What areas qualify for USDA loans?

More than you'd think! About 97% of U.S. land area qualifies, including many suburban communities. Generally, areas with populations under 35,000 are eligible. Use the USDA eligibility map online or ask us to check if your desired location qualifies.

Are there income limits for USDA loans?

Yes, your household income must be at or below 115% of the area median income. Limits vary by location and household size. For example, a family of four in many areas can earn up to $110,000+ and still qualify. We'll help you determine if you meet the income requirements.

What is USDA mortgage insurance and how much does it cost?

USDA loans have two mortgage insurance fees: a 1% upfront fee (rolled into your loan) and an annual fee of 0.35% (divided into monthly payments). This is significantly cheaper than FHA insurance at 0.85% or conventional PMI, which can be 0.5-1.5%.

Can I use a USDA loan to buy a fixer-upper?

The property must meet USDA's minimum property standards at the time of purchase. However, you can use a USDA 504 loan or USDA 502 loan with repair escrow for minor repairs. For major renovations, consider our 203k renovation loan program instead.

How long does USDA loan approval take?

USDA loans typically take 30-45 days to close. The process includes property eligibility verification and USDA underwriting approval. Working with an experienced lender like us can help streamline the process and avoid delays.
